Visualizing Singapore's Religious Population

A collaborative effort by Jian Xiang and Rachel

-

Singapore is growing year on year, mainly attributed by the citizen population growth. By June 2020, there were 3.52 million Singapore citizens (Strategy Group, 2020). According to the 2015 General Household Survey, majority of the Chinese population either practise Buddhism or has no religion, with about 21% belonging to the Catholic or Other Christians categories. On the other hand, while most Indians are Hindu, many among the Indian population are either Muslims or Christians. Perhaps the most interesting composition is that of the Malays, who almost exclusively belong to the Islam category. Over the years, although the citizen birth per Malay female citien is standing at one of the highest amongest the ethnic groups, it does not contribute to the largest fraction of Islams (ibid).

Much work went into planning and governance to cater to the religious diversity of the Singapore population with impartiality and neutrality, urban planners and policymakers have a role to help maintain public order between opposing groups, in which one way includes safeguarding land parcels for the various religious groups to conduct their religious activities (Neo, 2019). Thus, places of worship like temples, mosques and churches can be found in every town in Singapore.

In addition to allocating sufficient land to the various religious groups, especially in highly religious pluralistic society like Singapore, the harmony and co-existence of the different religious groups and Places of Worships (PWs) are equally important when there are plurality of these nodes. Therefore, this study aims to focus on spatial locations of PWs against the density of population that practices religiously.

1. Religious Composition by Ethnic Group

Hint: Hover over bars for proportion percentages

The stacked bar chart above compares the religious composition of the four main ethnic groups in Singapore (Chinese, Malays, Indians, Others). According to the 2015 General Household Survey, majority of the Chinese population belong to either the categories of Buddhism and No Religion, with about 21% belonging to Christianity. The Malay population almost exclusively belong to the Islam. While most of the Indians are Hindu, there are many Muslims and Christians among the Indian population as well.

From the map above, it can be seen that the Buddhist and Taoist populations are spread rather evenly across the island, with Ang Mo Kio, Hougang and Sengkang having the highest population proprtion. The Islam population generally live along the outer edges of the island, with Tampines and Woodlands having the highest proportions. For the Christians, the converse is true; they tend to live within the central region of Singapore, with Bukit Timah and Tanglin having the highest proportions.
